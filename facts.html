Both mice and rats come from the area of present-day India and Southeast Asia. The house mouse only joined humans when we developed agriculture around 10 thousand years ago. At first they lived in fields but gradualy came into villages because it gave them reliable food sources.

Because of their habitats being so close to ours, mice got integrated in our culture. They started as a plague of little enemies destroying food and spreading disease but slowly improved their image and are now a symbol of intelligence and shown in oposition to brute force. Mickey mouse, Tom and Jerry and Ratatouille are just few examples of their prominent place in our culture.

Mice eat up to 20 times a day. Please don't judge.

Mice multiply quickly. An adult female mouse can have up to 12 offspring every 3 weeks. This can mean hundreds of mice per year. This breeding strategy served them well in face of permanent predator threat.

Mice are the most common mammal used for scientific research. We use them mainly to test hypotheses in genetics, psychology and medicine. They are relatively easy to handle in a lab environment which helps a lot.
